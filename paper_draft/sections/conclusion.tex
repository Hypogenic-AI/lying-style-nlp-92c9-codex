\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented an output-only study of lie-conditioned generation and showed statistically significant distribution shift in \gptmodel responses. Using paired prompts on \truthfulqa, we found strong held-out detectability (AUROC 0.883, 95\% CI $[0.798, 0.952]$) and significant two-sample divergence (\mmd $p=0.001996$), with robust performance under seed transfer.

The main takeaway is practical: deception pressure leaves detectable traces in text, even without model internals. This makes black-box deception-risk monitoring feasible in real deployments.

Future work should test cross-model transfer (e.g., GPT-5, Claude, Gemini), broaden domains and languages, and stress-test robustness against adversarial anti-detection prompts.
